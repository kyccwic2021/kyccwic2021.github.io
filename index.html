Chloe M. Gooden

Professor of Public Speaking
Chloe M. Gooden is an Instructor, Advisor, and Mentor. She holds a Bachelors & Masters in Communications and has experience in teaching, advising, and mentoring, in Public Speaking, Speech Writing, Presentation Creation and Preparation, Interpersonal Communications, Conflict Resolution, Dealing with Emotions, and Interpersonal Relationships for 10+ years. Outside of her academia, she is an Author, Speaker, and Founder of Chloe M. Gooden Ministries, LLC that houses Caring Counselors Coalition 501(c)(3), Rubies Healing & Letting Go and After Miscarriage. She has been in ministry for over 10+ years and has published several books, including her new release The Break Up: How to Let Go of Your Ex, Heal, Forgive, and Move Forward, and has her own YouTube Channel, and Podcast; Convos with Chloe. In her messages, and ministry, she hopes to bring healing, guidance, hope, freedom, and encouragement that allows women to experience a beautiful life on earth + eternity in Heaven.

Amy Norrod

MIT Program Coordinator/Associate Professor
Amy Norrod is an Associate Professor in Medical Information Technology at SKYCTC. She holds degrees in administration, business management, and healthcare information management with 16 years of experience working within a medical facility. She also has 10 years of higher education experience both in the classroom and online format. She also serves as a program coordinator in her area of expertise and loves education! Her hobbies include exercise, mission trips, and advocating for children within the foster system as a CASA volunteer.

Evelyn Thrasher

Associate Dean, Gordon Ford College of Business
Evelyn Thrasher is Associate Dean and Professor of Information Systems in the Gordon Ford College of Business at Western Kentucky University. Evelyn joined the faculty of WKU in 2008 and has taught information technology and data analytics courses at both the undergraduate and graduate level. In her time at WKU, Evelyn has served as the Director of the GFCB Professional Education and Knowledge Program, the Knicely Faculty Fellow in the Center for Leadership Excellence, and the coordinator for AACSB accreditation. Evelyn earned a PhD in Management (2006) and an MBA (2003) from Auburn University and a BS in Mathematics (1989) from East Tennessee State University. Evelyn is a 2018 graduate of Leadership Kentucky and a 2018 graduate of the Harvard University Women in Education Leadership Institute. Prior to her career in academia, Evelyn was an Advanced Systems Analyst for Eastman Chemical Company from 1989-2000.
